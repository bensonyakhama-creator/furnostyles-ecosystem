## 3. Suggested Article Depth

### Three-Tier Content Architecture

| Tier | Type | Word Count | Purpose | Example |
|------|------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Tier 1** | Cornerstone Guides | 3,000–5,000 words | Rank for broad head terms. Become the definitive resource. Internally link to all supporting articles. | "The Complete Guide to Premium Interior Design in Kenya" |
| **Tier 2** | Supporting Guides | 1,500–2,500 words | Target long-tail queries. Deep-dive into specific subtopics. Link up to cornerstone. | "Gypsum Ceiling Design, Installation and Cost in Kenya" |
| **Tier 3** | Quick Tips Articles | 800–1,200 words | Answer specific questions. Target featured snippets. Link up to supporting guide or cornerstone. | "5 Quick Fixes for a Small Living Room in Nairobi" |

### Content Depth Rules

| Element | Cornerstone (Tier 1) | Supporting (Tier 2) | Quick Tips (Tier 3) |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| H2 headings | 6–10 | 4–6 | 3–4 |
| H3 headings | 12–20 | 6–12 | 2–4 |
| Images | 5–8 | 3–5 | 1–2 |
| Internal links | 8–12 | 5–8 | 2–4 |
| External links | 2–4 | 1–2 | 0–1 |
| FAQ items | 6–10 | 4–6 | 3–4 |
| CTAs | 3–4 | 2–3 | 1–2 |
| Case study mentions | 2–3 | 1–2 | 0–1 |
| Cost tables | Yes | Optional | No |
| Comparison tables | Yes | Optional | No |
| Video embeds | Placeholder | Optional | No |

## 6. Recommended Content Quality Standards

### 6.1 Long-Form Depth

| Metric | Standard | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Cornerstone word count** | 3,000–5,000 words | Outranks thin competitors. Signals topical authority. |
| **Supporting word count** | 1,500–2,500 words | Sufficient depth for long-tail ranking without fluff. |
| **Quick tips word count** | 800–1,200 words | Matches featured snippet length. Fast to produce. |
| **Information density** | Every 200 words must contain a fact, example, or actionable tip | Prevents filler. Maintains reader engagement. |
| **Original research** | Minimum 1 unique data point or original insight per cornerstone | Earns backlinks and citations. Differentiates from AI content. |

### 6.2 Headings

| Element | Rule | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **H1** | 1 per page. Contains primary keyword naturally. | "The Complete Guide to Premium Interior Design in Kenya" |
| **H2** | 6–10 for cornerstones, 4–6 for supporting, 3–4 for quick tips. Each must introduce a distinct subtopic. | "The Premium Planning Method", "Materials and Workmanship" |
| **H3** | Used under H2s to break subtopics into scannable sections. | "Budgeting in Layers", "Choosing the Right Contractor" |
| **Keyword in headings** | Primary keyword in 1 H1 and 2–3 H2s. LSI/variations in remaining H2s. | "Interior Design in Kenya" (H1), "Kenyan Interior Design Trends" (H2), "Nairobi Interior Design Costs" (H2) |
| **Heading hierarchy** | No skipped levels. H1 → H2 → H3 only. | Avoid H1 → H3 jumps. |
| **Heading style** | Sentence case or title case. Consistent across all articles. | "The Premium Planning Method" or "The premium planning method" |

### 6.3 Image Placement

| Rule | Standard | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Hero image** | 1 per article. 1200×800 minimum. WebP with JPEG fallback. | Social sharing, featured snippets, visual hook. |
| **Inline images** | 1 per 500 words minimum. Maximum 1 per 300 words (don't overcrowd). | Break up text, illustrate concepts, improve time on page. |
| **Image types** | Real project photos > diagrams > comparison tables > infographics > stock photos | Authenticity builds trust. Stock photos signal generic content. |
| **Alt text** | Descriptive, keyword-rich but natural. Under 125 characters. | SEO, accessibility, image search ranking. |
| **Captions** | Brief, contextual. Can include a soft keyword. | Extra keyword placement. Improves image context. |
| **Image compression** | WebP at 80% quality. Max file size 200KB for inline, 400KB for hero. | Page speed. Core Web Vitals. |
| **Lazy loading** | All below-the-fold images. Native `loading="lazy"`. | Faster initial paint. Better LCP scores. |

### 6.4 CTA Placement

| Location | CTA Type | Copy Examples |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| **After hero intro** | Soft, educational | "Not sure where to start? [Request a free consultation →]" |
| **After first major H2** | Contextual, relevant | "Want this look in your home? [See our interior services →]" |
| **After comparison/cost tables** | Hard, action-oriented | "Get a detailed quote for your project. [Contact us →]" |
| **Before FAQ** | Service-focused | "Ready to begin? [Explore our finishing packages →]" |
| **After FAQ** | Conversion-focused | "Still have questions? [Talk to our team →]" |
| **End of article** | Portfolio/reassurance | "See real projects like this. [View our portfolio →]" |
| **Sticky sidebar (desktop)** | Always-visible soft CTA | "Need help? [Request a call →]" |

**CTA design rules:**
- Primary CTA button: gold (`#d4af37`) background, dark text. High contrast.
- Secondary CTA: outline style, same colour.
- Minimum button height: 48px (touch-friendly).
- CTA text: action verb + benefit. "Get a quote" not "Submit".

### 6.5 Keyword Density

| Metric | Target | Ceiling | Rationale |
|--------|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Primary keyword density** | 1.0–1.5% | 2.0% | Natural reading flow. Avoids stuffing penalties. |
| **LSI/related keyword density** | 2.0–3.0% total | 4.0% | Signals topical breadth without repetition. |
| **Primary keyword in first 100 words** | Yes (exact or partial) | — | Early keyword placement signals relevance. |
| **Primary keyword in H1** | Yes (exact or close variation) | — | H1 is the strongest on-page signal. |
| **Primary keyword in URL slug** | Yes | — | URL is a ranking factor. |
| **Primary keyword in meta description** | Yes | — | Improves CTR from search results. |
| **Keyword in alt text** | 1 image per article max | — | Image SEO without over-optimisation. |

### 6.6 Readability

| Metric | Target | Tool/Method |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Flesch Reading Ease** | 50–70 (fairly easy to standard) | Hemingway Editor, Grammarly |
| **Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level** | 8–10 | Hemingway Editor |
| **Sentence length** | Average 15–20 words. Max 25 words. | Manual edit, Hemingway |
| **Paragraph length** | 3–5 sentences maximum. 1-sentence paragraphs allowed for emphasis. | Manual edit |
| **Active voice** | 80%+ of sentences | Grammarly, ProWritingAid |
| **Transition words** | Every 2–3 sentences | Yoast SEO, manual check |
| **Jargon** | Defined on first use. Minimised in introductions. | Manual edit |
| **Lists and tables** | At least 1 per 1,000 words | Improves scannability, featured snippet chances |
| **Bold text** | Used for key takeaways, important warnings, or summary sentences | Improves scannability |

| Articles with 90%+ duplicate content (only keyword changed) | Duplicate content penalty risk. Wastes crawl budget. | 301 redirect to the strongest version. Canonicalise if keeping any version. |
| Articles targeting misspelled keywords (e.g., "interior desing Kenya") | No search volume. No user value. | Remove. No redirect needed. |
| Outdated year-specific articles ("2024 Interior Design Trends") with no updates | Signals neglect. Poor user experience if trends are obsolete. | Update and merge into a current cornerstone, or remove. |
| Pure product description pages disguised as blog posts | Duplicate marketplace content. Thin value. | Remove. Redirect to `marketplace.html` when live. |
| Articles with no internal links to any service or contact page | Orphan pages with no conversion path. No SEO or business value. | 301 redirect to `services.html` or nearest cornerstone. |
| Articles where all images are broken and no alt text exists | Poor accessibility and UX. Signals low quality to Google. | Do not migrate. Rebuild from scratch if topic is in the 25. |
| Articles that are exact copies of manufacturer product pages | Duplicate content. No original value. | Remove. Redirect to manufacturer or marketplace when live. |
| "About Us" or "Company News" blog posts | Not SEO content. Belong on about page or social media. | Remove. No redirect needed. |
| Articles with no search demand (0 monthly searches) | Wastes crawl budget. No traffic potential. | Remove. No redirect needed. |

### 7.3 Retirement Process

1. **Audit:** Run Screaming Frog or equivalent to identify all old blog URLs.
2. **Map:** Assign each URL to one of: (a) merge into new article, (b) 301 redirect, (c) remove entirely.
3. **Implement redirects:** Use `.htaccess` (Apache) or server config (Nginx). For static hosting, use meta refresh or JS redirect as fallback.
4. **Update sitemap:** Remove retired URLs. Add new URLs with `lastmod` dates.
5. **Submit to Search Console:** Request re-crawl of updated sitemap.
6. **Monitor:** Check for 404 errors in Search Console for 4 weeks post-migration.
